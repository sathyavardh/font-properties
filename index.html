Font Properties

Poppins

Poppins is a geometric sans-serif typeface designed by Jonny Pinhorn and Ninad Kale, published by Indian Type Foundry. It is available in nine weights with matching italics, supporting both Latin and Devanagari languages. Poppins is suitable for a wide range of applications, including logos, branding, magazines, websites, and apps. It is also a good choice for body text, but its legibility may vary depending on the point size and tracking. For long-form content, Poppins may not be the best option due to its distinctive design and potential readability issues. While Poppins is a modern and legible font, it may not be the best choice for long reads at small point sizes due to its single-story “a” which can be mistaken for the letter “o”. Serif fonts are generally easier and faster to read for longer sections of body copy.

Dancing Script

"Dancing Script" is a popular cursive typeface designed by Pablo Impallari. It is characterized by its smooth, flowing style that mimics the look of handwriting, making it ideal for applications that require a touch of elegance and personalization.

  1. Cursive Style: The font emulates a modern script or handwritten style, with letters connected in a fluid manner.
  2. Variability: It includes multiple weights, allowing for variations in thickness to suit different design needs.
  3. Legibility: Despite its cursive nature, Dancing Script maintains high legibility, making it suitable for both display and body text.
  4. Playful and Elegant: It conveys a sense of playfulness and elegance, making it suitable for invitations, greeting cards, and creative branding.

Ga Maamli

The "Ga Maamli" font is a stylish and modern typeface known for its unique and contemporary design. This font is often used in graphic design and digital media for its distinctive look that combines both traditional and modern elements. Its elegant curves and clean lines make it suitable for various creative projects, such as branding, posters, and social media graphics.
